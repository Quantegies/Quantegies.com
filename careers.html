Hiring process

Rigorous, direct, and respectful.

The exact process varies by role, but every stage is designed to evaluate real judgment rather than rehearsed interview performance.

STEP 01

Application review

We review your work, experience, and evidence of ownership—not just titles or pedigree.

STEP 02

Working conversation

A focused discussion about your decisions, trade-offs, technical depth, and the role’s actual mandate.

STEP 03

Practical evaluation

A concise role-relevant exercise, case study, code review, research critique, or trading scenario.

STEP 04

Final alignment

We align on scope, expectations, compensation, availability, and what success looks like in the first 90 days.

FAQ

Before you apply.

Are all positions remote? +
Most roles are remote, but some positions have preferred time-zone overlap or location requirements. The role card and full description state the primary location clearly.
Do I need prior finance experience? +
Not for every role. For engineering and product positions, strong systems thinking and the ability to learn market concepts quickly may matter more. Quantitative research and trading roles generally require deeper market or statistical experience.
Can early-career candidates apply? +
Yes. We consider high-potential candidates who can demonstrate learning velocity, technical fundamentals, thoughtful projects, and strong accountability—even when their formal experience is limited.
What should I include with my application? +
Include a concise résumé or CV and links to relevant work. For research roles, papers, notebooks, or strategy write-ups are useful. For engineering roles, repositories, shipped products, architecture examples, or technical case studies are valuable.
How are salary ranges determined? +
Ranges reflect the role’s scope and primary hiring market. Final compensation depends on experience, location, employment structure, and the level at which the candidate is hired.
